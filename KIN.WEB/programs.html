How It Started

The Daily Devotional program was born out of a vision to help children establish a daily habit of connecting with God. In 2018, our founder noticed that many children lacked structured spiritual guidance outside of Sunday services. What began as a small WhatsApp group with 10 children has now grown into a global community with thousands of participants across multiple platforms.

Join Us Live

Our devotional is streamed live every day at 7:45 PM across these platforms:

Telegram Facebook YouTube

Each session includes scripture reading, interactive discussion, prayer time, and memory verse challenges. Children from different time zones can also access recorded sessions if they can't join live.

KidsInspiring Nation goDsCell

A weekly program to help children inculcate the tenets of Scripture as they learn, love & live the Word of God. Our goDsCell meetings create a community where children grow together in faith.

2 hours weekly
Small groups
Prayer & discussion

Our Beginnings

The goDsCell concept was inspired by the early church model in Acts 2:42-47. In 2019, we started with just 5 children meeting in a living room, sharing scriptures and praying together. Today, we have hundreds of goDsCells meeting across the nation, each led by trained child-leaders with adult mentors.

What Happens in a goDsCell?

Each goDsCell meeting follows this structure:

  • Welcome & Icebreaker (15 mins) - Fun activities to build relationships
  • Word Time (45 mins) - Interactive Bible study led by children
  • Prayer & Ministry (30 mins) - Children pray for each other and their communities
  • Action Challenge (30 mins) - Practical application of the week's lesson

The goDs University Concept

The Bible Challenge was launched in 2020 as a spiritual "university" where children systematically study through the entire Bible. Just like a university has different faculties and courses, our Bible University is structured to give children a comprehensive theological education.

University Structure

Year 1: Foundation Courses

  • Pentateuch Studies (Genesis-Deuteronomy)
  • Historical Books Survey
  • Wisdom Literature
  • Major Prophets

Year 1: Core Courses

  • Minor Prophets
  • Gospels & Acts
  • Pauline Epistles
  • General Epistles

Year 2: Specializations

  • Biblical Theology
  • Apologetics
  • Biblical Leadership
  • Missions & Evangelism

Year 2: Practical Ministry

  • Teaching Practicum
  • Discipleship Training
  • Worship Ministry
  • Community Outreach

Graduation Requirements

To graduate from the Bible University, children must:

  • Complete reading the entire Bible with study notes
  • Memorize 100 key scriptures
  • Teach at least 5 Bible lessons to peers
  • Complete a practical ministry project
  • Write a personal statement of faith

Graduates receive a special certificate and are eligible to become junior Bible teachers in our programs.
